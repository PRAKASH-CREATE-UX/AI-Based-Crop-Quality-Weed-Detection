About CottonWeeds

{% comment %} Weed Info Boxes {% endcomment %}
Carpetweed

A low-growing, mat-forming weed commonly found in cotton fields, difficult to control due to its rapid spreading ability.

Crabgrass

An aggressive grassy weed that thrives in disturbed soil, known for competing strongly with young cotton plants.

Eclipta

Often called false daisy, Eclipta is a broadleaf weed affecting cotton productivity, particularly in moist soils.

Goosegrass

A fast-spreading annual grass weed with a low rosette form, resistant to some herbicides used in cotton farming.

Morningglory

This twining vine wraps around cotton plants, interfering with harvesting and lowering crop quality significantly.

Nutsedge

Also known as nutgrass, this perennial weed reproduces via tubers and outcompetes cotton for water and nutrients.

Pricklysida

A small-leaved weed with bristly stems, commonly found in southern cotton fields and difficult to control post-emergence.

Purslane

A succulent broadleaf weed that grows in hot conditions, spreading easily and competing aggressively with cotton seedlings.

Ragweed

This allergenic weed affects both health and agriculture, reducing cotton yields and contaminating harvests.

Spurrendanoda

A spreading weed often mistaken for other broadleaf plants, it interferes with cotton canopy and competes for nutrients.
